\begin{tablenotes}
  \footnotesize
  \item[a] $\bar{\sigma}$ denotes the 67th percentile of the training-period
    (Jan~2000--Dec~2015) VSTOXX level, used as the excess-volatility
    threshold.  Only VSTOXX above $\bar{\sigma}$ reduces the equity weight;
    below-average volatility has no effect.
  \item \textit{Note:} Parameters are selected from the Bayesian-optimisation
    Pareto front (300 trials; TPE sampler; 6 Lagrangian penalty values
    $\lambda \in \{0,1,5,25,100,500\}$) by the pre-committed rule
    $\arg\min_{\theta \in \mathcal{P}} \lvert
    \mathbb{E}[\tilde{d}(\theta)] - 0.1394 \rvert$,
    where $0.1394$ is the DRL agent's total distribution on the historical
    test path.  The selected candidate delivers
    $\mathbb{E}[\tilde{d}] = 0.0846$ and $\hat{P}(\text{depletion}) = 23.1\%$
    over 1{,}000 simulated VAR paths.
    $(\cdot)^{+} \equiv \max(0, \cdot)$.  Equity weight is clipped to
    $[0.30,\;0.80]$; fill and distribution rates are clipped to $[0,\;0.10]$
    and $[0,\;0.05]$ respectively (Art.~10d constraints).
\end{tablenotes}
